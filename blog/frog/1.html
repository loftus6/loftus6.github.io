This Frog Does Not Exist: A Deep Dive into AI fakes

By: Neil Loftus 4/1/2024

In 2019, the website thispersondoesnotexist.com debuted, instantly making waves. Navigating to the page presents users with an endless stream of realistics faces. True to the name, not a single one of these faces of a real person. Rather, they were all generated by artifical intelligence (AI). The general reaction to this site was a mix of wonder and fear. News outlets reporting on the topic expressed a concern around possible misuse. While in 2019, this was a novel fear, in 2024 with the advent of chatGPT and other powerful generative AI applications had made concerns about "deepfakes" and AI misue palpable.

In this article, I wish to explore thispersondoesnotexist and other sites hosting nonexistant things. I will briefly touch on the technology that makes these tools possible, generative adversarial networks (GANs). Finally, I will attempt to implement my own fake object generator, in the vein of this "person does not exist", which I have deemed thisfrogdoesnot exist.
