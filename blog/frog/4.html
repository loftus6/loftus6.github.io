AI Frogs

After approximately 12 hours of training on my laptop, I was able to produce a GAN model that would generate frog images. If one had forgotten to put on their glasses before reading this blog, they would likely be quite impressed with these results. This person would of course be underwhelmed as soon as they did find their glasses. While my model does capture the basic shapes well, closer inspection reveals these creatures to be more reminiscent of lovecraftian horrors than natural frogs. Facial details are almost completely absent and there is a prevalent graininess to the images produced. While I was previously dismayed to find that thisfrogdoesnotexist.com already existed, the opportunity to compare my results with bitrate16â€™s was an appealing silver lining. Fortunately, they had also used the exact same dataset as me, making the comparison straightforward. While their images also have an element of strangeness to them, I consider them to be superior to my own images. This is apparent from the image resolution and finer details such as the presence of facial features. Comparing my results with theirs, I believe that given more training time I would eventually reach a similar level of believability.
